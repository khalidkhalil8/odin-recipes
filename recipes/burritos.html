Description

Mac and cheese is a warm, cheesy comfort food made by combining tender pasta with a rich, creamy cheese sauce. The sauce is prepared by melting cheddar into a smooth milk-and-butter base, creating a perfect coating for every bite. For extra flavor and texture, it can be baked until golden with a crispy breadcrumb topping. Whether served as a quick stovetop dish or baked for a special occasion, mac and cheese is a simple yet satisfying meal loved by all ages.

Ingredients

  • 8 ounces elbow macaroni
  • 2 cups shredded sharp cheddar cheese
  • 2 cups milk
  • 2 tablespoons butter
  • 2 tablespoons all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/4 teaspoon black pepper
  • 1/2 teaspoon mustard powder (optional)
  • 1/2 cup breadcrumbs (optional, for topping)

Steps

  1. Cook the elbow macaroni according to the package instructions, then drain and set aside.
  2. In a medium saucepan, melt the butter over medium heat.
  3. Whisk in the flour and cook for 1-2 minutes, stirring constantly, to form a roux.
  4. Gradually whisk in the milk, continuing to stir until the sauce thickens and begins to bubble.
  5. Remove from heat and stir in the shredded cheddar cheese until fully melted and smooth.
  6. Season the sauce with salt, black pepper, and mustard powder if using.
  7. Combine the cooked macaroni with the cheese sauce, stirring until evenly coated.
  8. If baking, transfer the mixture to a greased baking dish, sprinkle breadcrumbs on top, and bake at 350°F (175°C) for 20-25 minutes until golden and bubbly.
  9. Serve hot and enjoy.
